## üéØ PREVENTION STRATEGY

1. **Learn in Context** - Don't learn words in isolation!
2. **Create Associations** - Link false friends to their real meaning
3. **Use Mnemonics** - Make up memory tricks:
   - "EMBarazo = BABY" (Spanish embarazada = pregnant)
   - "CoNSTipado = NOSE/COLD" (not digestive system!)
4. **Practice With Examples** - Use real sentences, not just word lists
5. **Cross-Check** - Always verify against context before translating

---

## ‚úÖ BEST PRACTICES

When you encounter a word that looks familiar:
1. **STOP** - Don't assume it means the same as English
2. **VERIFY** - Check the context and meaning
3. **CONFIRM** - Look up in a reliable dictionary
4. **TEST** - Use in a sentence to make sure it makes sense
5. **REMEMBER** - Add to your personal false friends list

---

**Pro Tip:** The most dangerous false friends are those that are partially correct. For example, "sensible" in French DOES mean something about sense/feeling - it just means "sensitive" not "sensible." Always check!
